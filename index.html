About / Hackie3
Hackie3 is a distributed hacker collective building tools, breaking systems, and rewriting the rules of the grid.
Origin Signal
Born in dim basements and late-night IRC rooms, Hackie3 grew from a handful of curious intruders into a global swarm of exploit engineers, protocol designers and off-grid builders.
We move where networks are weakest and data is most valuable, orchestrating silent campaigns that challenge surveillance states, abusive platforms and brittle corporate stacks.
Red TeamReverse EngineeringCyberpunk
Mission / Directive
Expose fragile infrastructure, weaponize knowledge, and keep the stack weird. Hackie3 trains new operators, runs underground tooling drops, and executes coordinated operations against exploit-rich targets.
If there is a firewall, we pressure-test it. If there is a closed protocol, we tear it open. We document, we ship, and we leave glowing footprints in the logs.

Timeline / Lore
From a private channel with four ghosts to a distributed swarm of operators embedded across cities, datacenters and off-grid bunkers.
Milestones
2021 / Boot
Hackie3 channel created
Four operators, one private relay, and a shared drive full of half-finished exploits and cursed prototypes.
2023 / Amplify
First public CTF win
“Hackie3” appears on leaderboards, then in logs, then in SOC reports. Recruitment quietly accelerates.
2024 / Split
Cells go distributed
The collective fragments into semi-autonomous cells sharing implants, droppers and telemetry via encrypted dead-drops.
2025 / Signal Flood
Tooling leaks to the wild
Custom recon suites and privilege-escalation chains surface in random Git repos and pastebins, tagged only with “H3”.
Next Packet
